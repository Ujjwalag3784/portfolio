Product Thinking

How I Approach Problems

The mental models I use to turn ambiguity into shipped product.

01
Start with the Why
Before any solution, I map the problem space. For RewardTrust, this meant building a 160-entry dataset and defining a "Four Trust Gaps" framework before writing a single line of product spec. Understanding the problem deeply prevents building the wrong thing.
02
Define One North Star
Every product needs one clear metric that tells you if you're winning. For RewardTrust: Reward Discovery Rate. It aligns the entire team, resists metric vanity, and forces hard prioritization conversations. Without it, roadmaps become wishlists.
03
Prioritize Ruthlessly with RICE
Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort cuts through opinion-based debates. At GoKiwi, I saw how this framework enables fast, high-quality decisions in cross-functional teams where everyone has a different definition of "important."
04
Ship, Measure, Learn
Shipping is a hypothesis. I use Mixpanel to validate whether a feature did what the PRD said it would, then feed that learning into the next cycle. Analytics is what separates building products from building features.
